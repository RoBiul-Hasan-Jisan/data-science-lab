# ❤️ Cardiovascular Disease Prediction


## 📌 Project Overview
The **Cardiovascular Disease Prediction** project focuses on building a machine learning model to estimate the likelihood of cardiovascular disease in patients based on their medical history, lifestyle, and demographic factors.

By analyzing key health indicators, the model identifies high-risk individuals and supports early prevention strategies. This helps healthcare professionals make data-driven decisions and improve patient outcomes.

---

## 📊 Dataset Overview
The dataset contains multiple health-related attributes describing patient conditions and lifestyle habits. These features are used to predict cardiovascular disease risk.

---

## 📚 Data Dictionary

| Feature | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| General Health | Self-reported overall health status |
| Checkup | Time since last medical checkup |
| Exercise | Whether the patient exercises regularly |
| Heart Disease | Existing heart disease condition |
| Skin Cancer | Presence of skin cancer |
| Other Cancer | Presence of other cancers |
| Depression | History of depression |
| Diabetes | Diabetes status |
| Arthritis | Presence of arthritis |
| Sex | Gender of the patient |
| Age Category | Age group of the patient |
| BMI | Body Mass Index |
| Smoking History | History of smoking |
| Alcohol Consumption | Alcohol intake level |
| Fruit Consumption | Frequency of fruit intake |
| Green Vegetable Consumption | Frequency of green vegetables |
| Fried Potato Consumption | Frequency of fried food intake |

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Age is a major risk factor, especially for individuals above 55.
- Higher BMI increases the likelihood of cardiovascular disease.
- Regular exercise generally reduces risk, but intensity and age context matter.
- Healthy diet (fruits & vegetables) reduces risk, while fried foods increase it.
- Smoking history is a strong predictor of cardiovascular disease.
- Some conditions like diabetes, arthritis, cancer, and depression showed weak correlation in this dataset.
